This note summarizes the historical development from Clockwork Cosmology V1 to TRM/TQM V3.3 in a reviewer-safe, claim-bounded form.

## 1. Origin of the variable time-rate idea

In V1, the conceptual origin is a variable local time-rate factor as the driver of gravitational-like behavior in a flat-space coordinate picture.  
The early formulation used a direct time-rate-gradient acceleration expression and treated local timing structure as the core organizing variable.

Reviewer-safe interpretation: V1 establishes an initial hypothesis direction, not a closed proof framework.

---

## 2. Transition from Time-Aether language to scalar \(T(x,t)\) / TRM

From V1 to V2.2, the language shifts from "Time-Aether" wording toward a scalar temporal-rate field description \(T(x,t)\), later framed as TRM.  
This transition is methodologically important: it moves from broad conceptual narrative toward field-style parametrization and later test workflows.

Reviewer-safe interpretation: this is a formalization and reframing step, not a theorem-level derivation.

---

## 3. Emergence of the galactic boundary relation

In the V2.2-to-V3 path, galactic rotation analysis consolidates around the low-acceleration boundary relation
\[
g_{\mathrm{eff}} = g_{\mathrm{bar}} + \sqrt{g_{\mathrm{bar}}\,a_0}
\]
used in SPARC/RAR test workflows with explicit calibration, diagnostics, and later no-refit/holdout discipline.  
In current test discipline, this relation is treated as an effective weak-field/low-acceleration comparator and validated through guarded regression-style tests.

Reviewer-safe interpretation: empirical/tested-effective fit behavior in bounded regimes; not a fundamental uniqueness proof and not a claim that dark matter is disproven.

---

## 4. Shift from broad claims to test-gated candidate discipline

By V3.0 and later, the project posture changes clearly:

1. explicit guard tests and ablations,
2. fit/freeze/holdout and robustness stress checks,
3. explicit claim-boundary statements in docs and tests.

The emphasis moves from global narrative claims to sector-wise candidate status with falsification pressure.

Reviewer-safe interpretation: stronger scientific discipline and traceability, while keeping major theory claims open.

---

## 5. Placement of memory/action closure

V3.0 introduced a stronger memory-channel path; V3.1 hardened it via action-closure checks; V3.2 extended to minimal-action-from-lattice candidate support.

- V3.1 note position: action-derived memory closure candidate strengthened by independent guards, still not first-principles closure.
- V3.2 note position: minimal effective action plausibly lattice-motivated under comparative guards, still not theorem-level closure.

Reviewer-safe interpretation: the memory/action track is now a structured candidate chain with better internal consistency, not a completed derivation.

---

## 6. Placement of rational mode-locking and \(m=3\)

The mode-locking line evolves from rational-band competitiveness to explicit three-constraint gating (phase closure + bridge occupancy + action/tick consistency).  
Current V3.3 interpretation (RBF24–RBF26, documented in `TRM_M3_Bounded_Bridge_Mode_Note.md`):

- `m=3` appears as a **bounded bridge-mode candidate** under the full stack.
- robustness is substantial but not absolute (RBF25),
- transition/failure boundaries appear under stronger phase stress (RBF26).
- the key interpretation is joint-constraint dependence: `m=3` is selected under the full phase-closure, bridge-occupancy, and action/tick stack, while the earlier RBF chain indicates weaker or collapsed unique `m=3` selection when action/tick consistency is removed.

Reviewer-safe interpretation: bounded candidate selection behavior, not a proof of fundamental \(m=3\) necessity.

---

## 7. Claim-boundary evolution

Across V1 → V3.3, claim boundaries become progressively stricter and more explicit:

- from broad conceptual framing (V1),
- to field-formalized effective framework (V2.2),
- to guarded multi-sector candidate discipline (V3.0+),
- to explicit "candidate-only / not theorem-level / not first-principles closure" status in V3.1–V3.3 notes.

Explicit non-claims preserved in this lineage:

1. no theorem-level claims,
2. no GR replacement claim,
3. no claim that V1/V2 prove \(m=3\),
4. no claim that dark matter or dark energy are disproven.

---

## 8. Reviewer-safe summary

The V1→V3.3 trajectory is best read as a maturation from a variable-time-rate hypothesis into a guarded, test-driven effective-framework program.  
V3.1/V3.2 strengthen memory/action closure as candidate structure; V3.3 places \(m=3\) as a bounded three-constraint bridge-mode candidate with detected transition boundaries.

Net status: stronger falsification-aware candidate framework with improved internal discipline, while major first-principles/theorem-level questions remain open.
