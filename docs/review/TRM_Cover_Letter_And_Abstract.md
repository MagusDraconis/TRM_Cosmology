## Abstract (Draft)

We present the current status of the Temporal Rate Matrix / Temporal Quantum Matrix framework as a weak-field effective transport and synchronization model. The framework replaces direct curvature-first assumptions with an effective transport structure involving a scalar temporal-rate sector, a rotational vector candidate sector, and a nonlocal Theta observable sector.

Photon transport is modeled through an effective index
\[
n_{\mathrm{eff}} =
2+\lambda_t\phi+\lambda_s\phi^2|\dot{\mu}|,
\]
with tests supporting the memory channel as a directional transport term rather than a pure time-delay correction. A microscopic lattice-proxy path supports the emergence of \(\phi^2|\dot{\mu}|\) up to an effective coupling scale.

A rational collective mode-locking path supports an inverse band
\[
\Omega \approx 1.16..1.19,
\quad
\gamma = 1/\Omega \approx 0.84..0.86,
\]
with \(m=3\) identified as a strongly constrained closure-order candidate under phase, direction, and action/tick constraints.

For galactic dynamics, a nonlocal Theta observable path
\[
\Theta \rightarrow O_5 \rightarrow \lambda_\Theta \rightarrow g_{\mathrm{obs}}
\]
is developed as a tested-effective candidate, with holdout, leakage, anti-proxy, and lattice-energy consistency guards.

A new vector sector introduces \(\vec A_T\) and \(\vec B_T=\nabla\times\vec A_T\) as a weak-field frame-dragging candidate, reproducing Lense-Thirring-like scaling behavior structurally and supporting a non-fitted effective coupling path.

Finally, a unified effective-action roadmap links the scalar, vector, and Theta sectors while preserving all known sector limits. The current work remains below theorem-level first-principles closure and does not claim equivalence with General Relativity.
