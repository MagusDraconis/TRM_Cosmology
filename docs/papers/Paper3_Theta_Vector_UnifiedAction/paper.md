# Theta, Vector, and Unified-Action Constraints in TRM/TQM V3.0

## Abstract

This paper isolates the TRM/TQM V3.0 theta-observable path, vector frame-dragging candidate sector, and unified effective-action roadmap. The objective is to state what is currently tested-effective, what is structurally constrained, and what remains open at theorem level. The chain \(\Theta \rightarrow O_5 \rightarrow \lambda_\Theta \rightarrow g_{\mathrm{obs}}\) is strongly supported by guard tests; the vector sector is strongly hardened in weak-field candidate form through FD01-FD20; and unified-action guards UF01-UF09 preserve sector limits and bounded cross-couplings. These results remain below theorem-level first-principles closure and do not claim GR replacement.

## 1. Introduction

TRM/TQM V3.0 uses sector-wise hardening and cross-sector consistency guards to constrain model freedom while keeping claim boundaries explicit. This paper focuses on the three tracks that define current cross-sector structure:

1. the nonlocal theta-observable chain,
2. the vector rotational/frame-dragging candidate sector,
3. the unified effective-action roadmap that links scalar, vector, and theta sectors.

## 2. Theta-Observable Path

### 2.1 Core chain

\[
\Theta \rightarrow O_5 \rightarrow \lambda_\Theta \rightarrow g_{\mathrm{obs}}.
\]

### 2.2 Current test support

The theta path is currently hardened by TO01-TO28, TQK01-TQK04, LC01-LC08, and TOL01-TOL04. At this stage, the chain is treated as tested-effective with strong guard-level support for operator behavior, holdout discipline, anti-proxy safeguards, and lattice-energy consistency.

### 2.3 Claim boundary

- Supported: strongly constrained theorem path at tested-effective level.
- Not claimed: theorem-level microscopic closure of the full \(\Theta \rightarrow g_{\mathrm{obs}}\) map.

## 3. Vector Frame-Dragging Candidate Sector

### 3.1 Core structure

The vector sector is based on:
\[
\vec A_T,\qquad \vec B_T=\nabla\times\vec A_T,
\]
with weak-field frame-dragging candidate interpretation.

### 3.2 Current hardening status

FD01-FD20 currently support:

1. rotational source generation and spin-sign reversal behavior,
2. weak-field scaling shape and null/spin-zero fallback consistency,
3. derived non-fitted effective \(k_T\) normalization discipline,
4. ablation/holdout/unit-scaling robustness,
5. controlled systematic-bias auditing under no-refit constraints.

### 3.3 Claim boundary

- Supported: tested-effective weak-field frame-dragging candidate sector.
- Not claimed: theorem-level first-principles closure or full quantitative GR-equivalence.

## 5. Cross-Sector Falsification Criteria

The current theta/vector/unified interpretation should be narrowed or rejected if:

1. theta-chain anti-proxy or holdout guarantees collapse under independent tests;
2. vector weak-field guard behavior fails under robust ablation/benchmark stress;
3. unified-action interaction terms break sector-limit recovery or MC/FD/TO guard preservation;
4. claimed cross-coupling structure requires hidden per-dataset re-normalization.

## 6. Remaining First-Principles Gaps

Open items remain explicit:

- theorem-level microscopic closure for the theta chain,
- theorem-level first-principles closure for vector-sector normalization and benchmark mapping,
- theorem-level closure of unified cross-sector interaction structure.

## 7. Conclusion

In V3.0, theta, vector, and unified-action blocks are strongly constrained at tested-effective level and form a coherent candidate multi-sector framework under explicit guard discipline. The status is review-ready and falsifiable, but it is not theorem-level complete and does not claim replacement of General Relativity.
